Who We Are

The Devastating Delta

Delta is the third chapter chartered in La Hermandad's history — established April 24, 1988, spanning the University of Pennsylvania, Drexel, Temple, and Haverford. For nearly four decades, the chapter has operated at the center of Philadelphia's Latinx student life: co-founding the Latinx Coalition, helping bring La Casa Latina into existence, and building the institutions that came after us.

Membership is a standard, not a slogan. We hold each other to it in the classroom, in the community, and long after graduation.

Our History

From Cornell
to Philadelphia

February 19, 1982

La Hermandad Founded

13 visionaries establish Lambda Upsilon Lambda at Cornell University — the only Latino fraternity to charter at all eight Ivy League schools.

April 24, 1988

Devastating Delta Chartered

Delta becomes the third chapter in LUL history, established across Penn, Drexel, Temple, and Haverford — one of the most expansive chapters in the fraternity.

1991

Delta's Influence Spreads

Brothers from Rutgers travel to Penn for Delta's "Colors" program and are so moved they bring LUL to New Jersey, founding the Iota Chapter. Delta's legacy expanded La Hermandad nationally.

1996

Building the Infrastructure

Delta hermanos help unify Penn's Latinx student groups into the Latinx Coalition — and co-found Onda Latina, Penn's first Latin dance troupe — laying institutional groundwork that still stands today.

1999

La Casa Latina Opens

The Latino Student Coalition and Latino Faculty & Staff Association's proposal becomes reality: Penn's Center for Hispanic Excellence — La Casa Latina — is inaugurated, with Delta among the organizations that helped build the case for it.

Annually

Pa'Lante Banquet

Delta's flagship event, started to give hermanos who couldn't travel home for the holidays a place to belong. Now in its 34th year — proceeds fund the chapter's Brown and Gold Christmas Initiative.

Today

The Standard Continues

Active across Philadelphia's campuses, connecting undergrads and alumni across industries, cities, and generations. This is what Delta was built for.

Becoming a Hermano?

If you're a student at Penn, Drexel, Temple, or Haverford and you feel called to something bigger — here's how it starts.

1

Learn Who We Are

Read up on La Unidad Latina, our four pillars, and what Delta Chapter has meant to Philadelphia since 1988. Start at launidadlatina.org.

2

Follow & Show Up Online

Follow @lul_devastatingdelta on Instagram. Engage. Let us see who you are before you reach out — presence matters.

3

Come to an Interest Meeting

We host rush events and interest meetings every semester. No commitment — just come, meet the chapter, ask your questions, and feel La Hermandad for yourself.

4

Submit Your Interest Form

Apply through Penn Clubs (pennclubs.com) or email lul.devastatingdelta@gmail.com. A current hermano will be in contact.

5

Go Through the Process

Membership Education is rigorous and meaningful. It's not easy on purpose — it's designed to build brothers. Come ready.
